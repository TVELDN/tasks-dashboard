🌍 Morning Markets Briefing — Sunday, 21 June 2026
Top Story

A synchronized global tightening wave defined the week: the Fed held at 3.50–3.75% but issued a hawkish pivot under new Chair Kevin Warsh (9 of 18 officials pencilling in a hike, median FFR raised to 3.8%), the ECB hiked +25bp on June 11 (first since 2023), and the BoJ raised to 1.00% on June 16 — its highest rate since 1995. Simultaneously, US-Iran peace talks collapsed after follow-up negotiations in Switzerland were abruptly cancelled, leaving oil elevated above $92/bbl and risk assets on edge heading into the week of June 22.

Central Banks & Rates
  • Fed (June 18): Held at 3.50–3.75% (unanimous). Removed forward guidance on cuts. Median 2026 dot raised to 3.80% from 3.40%; 9 of 18 officials see ≥1 hike. Warsh explicitly prioritised price stability over growth support.
  • ECB (June 11): +25bp — deposit facility to 2.25%, MRO 2.40%. First hike since 2023. Inflation baseline: 3.0% in 2026. Driver: persistent energy shock from Middle East/Strait of Hormuz. Markets price ≥1 additional hike by year-end.
  • BoJ (June 16): +25bp to 1.00%, highest since 1995 (7-1 vote). Japan wholesale CPI +6.3% in May. Yen weakened to 160.22 vs USD despite the hike — rate differentials with the US still overwhelmingly favour the dollar.
  • Swiss National Bank: Held rates unchanged. Switzerland revised 2026 GDP growth forecast down to 0.9% (from 1.0%) citing energy headwinds.
Equities
  • US (Fri June 20 close): S&P 500 ~7,500 (+1.08% Thursday); Nasdaq 100 +2.5% Thursday on AI/chip momentum. Week was volatile — sharp selloff June 17 on FOMC hawkish dot-plot, partially recovered on chipmaker rebound.
  • Asia: Nikkei 225 +0.8% at record high; South Korea Kospi +3.1%, supported by AI semiconductor optimism. China/HK closed mid-week.
  • Europe: Mixed to negative Friday as Iran deal durability questioned. CAC 40 underperformed peers; ECB hike and French fiscal concerns weighed on sentiment.
  • Tech leadership: Top S&P 500 weights — Nvidia (7.9%), Apple (6.8%), Alphabet (6.0%), Microsoft (4.4%), Amazon (3.7%) — continue to anchor index performance.
FX & Commodities
  • USD: Broad strength post-FOMC. EUR/USD fell to ~$1.145, weakest since mid-March, on track for -1% weekly loss. USD/JPY at multi-year highs despite BoJ hike. AUD and NOK favoured by strategists as commodity-linked alternatives.
  • Oil: Brent above $92/bbl, WTI near $90. Elevated on Middle East supply risk premium. Iran talk cancellation renewed fears over Strait of Hormuz. Lower oil briefly priced in on ceasefire hopes — that trade has unwound.
  • Gold: Fell to ~$4,106/oz — third consecutive weekly decline. Hawkish central banks and USD strength reducing appeal of non-yielding metals. Structural CB demand provides a floor.
  • JGBs: 10-year JGB yield climbed to 2.615% after BoJ hike. Spread dynamics between JGBs and USTs/Bunds remain a key driver of global duration positioning.
Macro Data & Politics
  • US-Iran talks collapsed: Follow-up negotiations in Switzerland abruptly cancelled, reversing tentative weekend ceasefire optimism. Strait of Hormuz risk premium not fully priced out; oil and European equities vulnerable to further deterioration.
  • G7 in Evian-les-Bains (France): Ukraine war, US tariffs (10–12.5% threatened on EU and 60 trading partners), and Iran-US tensions on agenda. Trade friction between US and G7 partners remains a macro overhang.
  • US inflation trajectory: Fed raised 2026 inflation forecasts. PCE data Thursday June 25 is the next key reading — Wells Fargo forecasts headline +4.1% YoY, core +3.4% YoY. A hot print could cement the hike path.
  • Eurozone: ECB projects headline inflation averaging 3.0% in 2026. Energy cost pass-through to core still materialising. Money markets now price ≥1 more ECB hike this year.
From Le Figaro
  • G7 summit hosted by France: Evian-les-Bains summit puts France at the centre of global macro diplomacy. Ukraine aid, US tariff threats on EU goods, and Iran all on the agenda — outcomes could move EUR and European equities early next week.
  • Choose France momentum: The June 1 summit attracted €93bn of investment commitments and 15,600 jobs — seventh consecutive year France tops European FDI rankings. Supports the narrative of French competitiveness despite fiscal headwinds.
  • Tech sovereignty push: European Commission launched a "Tech Sovereignty Package" (June 3), driven significantly by French pressure. Aimed at reducing reliance on US digital infrastructure — potential regulatory implications for US tech names with EU exposure.
What to Watch Today
  • Monday June 22 — US market reopen: First session after the FOMC hawkish pivot and Iran talk collapse. Catch-up price action expected; watch for any weekend Iran/G7 headlines that shift the tone.
  • Fed speakers (week of June 22): Multiple FOMC officials scheduled post-meeting. Chair Warsh's first post-hike-signal remarks will be closely watched for rate path calibration.
  • Thursday June 25 — PCE inflation: May PCE and core PCE (Fed's preferred inflation gauge). Consensus: headline ~4.1% YoY, core ~3.4% YoY. A hot print hardens the case for a 2026 hike; a miss opens a small window for a hold. Also: Germany Ifo Business Survey and Canada CPI this week.
