🌍 Morning Markets Briefing — Saturday, 20 June 2026
Top Story

The Federal Reserve held rates at 3.50–3.75% but signalled a sharp hawkish pivot under new Chair Kevin Warsh — removing forward guidance on cuts, raising the 2026 median FFR projection to 3.8%, with nine of 18 officials pencilling in at least one hike. The ECB separately delivered its first rate increase since 2023 (+25bp, deposit rate to 2.25% on 11 June), driven by energy-led inflation from Iran/Strait of Hormuz disruptions. Both major central banks leaning tighter simultaneously — a meaningful shift in the global policy backdrop.

Central Banks & Rates
  • Fed: Rates held at 3.50–3.75% (unanimous). Dot plot raised median 2026 FFR to 3.80% (from 3.40% in March). Rate-cut language removed entirely. Chair Warsh emphasised price stability; markets pricing ~1 hike by year-end.
  • ECB (11 June): +25bp — deposit facility 2.25%, MRO 2.40%, marginal lending 2.65%. First hike since 2023. Inflation forecast: 3.0% (2026), 2.3% (2027), 2.0% (2028). Driver: persistent energy shock from Middle East conflict.
  • Bank of Japan: Rate raised to 0.75%, highest in three decades. Yen still weakening despite hike; BoJ FX intervention risk remains live. Negative real rates maintain structural yen pressure.
Equities
  • US (last close Wed 18 June): S&P 500 ~5,968, down ~0.22%. Sharp sell-off on 17 June after FOMC dot-plot hawkish surprise; triple-witching expiry added volatility. Markets closed 19 June (Juneteenth); reopen 22 June.
  • Europe (Fri 19 June): Stoxx 600 -0.2%, FTSE 100 -0.35%, CAC 40 -0.55%. Weakness on scepticism over Iran peace deal durability after follow-up talks postponed.
  • Asia-Pacific: Broadly lower Friday, tracking Iran deal uncertainty and JPY cross-asset volatility from BoJ's hike.
  • Outlook: JP Morgan maintains double-digit 2026 global equity return forecast (DM & EM) but recommends increasing hedges on US equities and maintaining safeguards in Europe.
FX & Commodities
  • Oil: Brent ~$101/bbl (early June peak); WTI -8.9% and Brent -6.3% off June highs following ceasefire. Supply risk premium partially unwound but fragile — deal durability in question.
  • EUR/USD: Euro under pressure from oil import cost shock and ECB/Fed policy dynamics. Short EUR vs AUD/NOK favoured by macro strategists.
  • JPY: Yen continues to weaken despite BoJ hike to 0.75%. FX intervention and jawboning remain live risks.
  • Gold: Strong structural support entering June; precious metals broadly higher on risk-off flows and central bank diversification demand.
Macro Data & Politics
  • Iran/Middle East: US-brokered peace deal announced but immediately questioned — postponement of follow-up Washington-Tehran talks sent European and Asian equities lower Friday. Strait of Hormuz risk premium not fully priced out.
  • US inflation: Fed raised inflation forecasts; price stability explicitly prioritised under Warsh. Energy cost pass-through to CPI expectations elevated.
  • Eurozone inflation: ECB projects 3.0% headline average in 2026, well above target. Banque de France June macroeconomic projections published this week.
  • BoJ paradox: Rate at 30-year high yet yen weakening — structural FX dynamics at play beyond rate differentials alone.
From Le Figaro
  • France budget enacted: After two failed no-confidence motions, France's 2026 budget passed under PM Lecornu. Targets 5.0% deficit/GDP (vs. 5.4% in 2025), with €30bn+ fiscal squeeze, €6.5bn defence spending rise, corporate tax break cuts, and tighter welfare contribution rules.
  • Fiscal credibility risk: Banque de France Governor Villeroy de Galhau warned the deficit must not exceed 4.8% to stay on track for the EU 3% target by 2029. Fiscal watchdog flagged optimistic revenue assumptions.
  • Business impact: Tax-heavy budget expected to weigh on French investment and hiring in 2026. OAT spreads to Bunds remain sensitive to execution slippage; CAC 40 underperformed peers Friday.
ETFs & Passive Flows
  • Crypto ETFs — heavy outflows: US spot Bitcoin ETFs posted a record $1.72B in net outflows for the week ending 6 June (13-day cumulative: $4.4B), led by BlackRock IBIT (-$1.34B). Ethereum ETFs also under pressure (-$168M weekly). Driver: hawkish Fed reducing appeal of non-yielding assets vs bonds.
  • Equity ETFs — resilient inflows: VOO (Vanguard S&P 500) leads YTD with $75.7B in inflows; passive equity ETFs account for ~68% of total US ETF inflows. Broad index products continue attracting consistent flows despite market volatility.
  • Fixed income ETFs — structural shift: $163B into fixed income ETFs YTD through April (30% of total ETF flows); active fixed income ETFs capturing ~$62B of that. Rate uncertainty driving demand for flexible duration strategies.
  • Europe (week 23): European-listed equity ETFs attracted €7.65B in the first week of June — largest allocation across all asset classes. Fixed income ETFs gathered €2.59B. Risk-on rotation into equities despite ECB hike.
  • Industry backdrop: Passive ETF market valued at ~$16.8T globally in 2026. Active ETF share growing fast, particularly in fixed income where active strategies now rival passive on flow momentum.
What to Watch Today
  • Monday 22 June — US market reopen: Catch-up price action expected on the FOMC hawkish signal and Friday's geopolitical developments. Watch for any Iran deal headlines over the weekend.
  • Fed speakers (week of 22 June): Multiple FOMC officials on the circuit post-meeting to clarify the rate hike path; Warsh remarks will be tone-setting for rates markets.
  • PMI flash estimates & Iran talks: US and eurozone PMI flash estimates due; any resumption or breakdown of Iran-Washington negotiations will move oil, EUR, and risk assets sharply.
